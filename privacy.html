Privacy Policy

How we handle your information

Last updated: April 2025  |  Effective date: April 1, 2025

Who we are

BeyondA1C is a program of the Boston Diabetes Research Institute, a division of MedicaMetrix, Inc. ("BeyondA1C," "we," "our," or "us"). We operate the website at beyonda1c.com and related digital properties. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and protect information when you visit our website or interact with us.

Information we collect

We collect information in several ways depending on how you interact with us:

  • Information you provide directly: When you fill out our provider inquiry form, send us an email, or request a consultation, we collect your name, organization, email address, phone number, and any other details you choose to share.
  • Usage information: When you visit our website, we automatically collect technical information such as your IP address, browser type, pages visited, time spent on pages, referring URL, and device information. This is collected through cookies and similar technologies.
  • Communications: If you email us or correspond with us, we retain those communications to respond to your inquiry and improve our service.

How we use your information

  • To respond to your inquiry or consultation request
  • To provide information about BeyondA1C products and services
  • To send updates or educational content you have requested
  • To analyze website usage and improve the site experience
  • To comply with legal obligations
  • To protect the security and integrity of our website

How we share your information

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information in limited circumstances:

  • Service providers: We work with third-party vendors (such as website hosting, email, and analytics providers) who process data on our behalf under strict confidentiality agreements.
  • MedicaMetrix affiliates: As a division of MedicaMetrix, we may share information with affiliated entities for operational purposes.
  • Legal requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or to protect the rights and safety of our organization or others.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction.

Data retention

We retain personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, unless a longer retention period is required by law. Inquiry and contact form submissions are typically retained for up to three years. Website analytics data is retained in aggregated form.

Security

We use industry-standard technical and organizational measures to protect your information, including encrypted data transmission (HTTPS), access controls, and regular security reviews. No method of transmission over the internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Third-party links

Our website contains links to external sites, including dpncheck.com, PubMed, and journal publishers. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their policies.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will post the revised version on this page with an updated effective date. Continued use of our website after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Questions about this policy? Contact our privacy team at privacy@beyonda1c.com or write to us at Boston Diabetes Research Institute, 103 Fox Road, Suite 611, Waltham, MA 02451.

HIPAA

Notice of Privacy Practices

Last updated: April 2025  |  Applies to: clinical and research partnerships involving protected health information

Important: This notice applies to situations where BeyondA1C or MedicaMetrix receives protected health information (PHI) as a business associate of a covered healthcare entity. The BeyondA1C website itself does not collect health information from individual patients.

Our role under HIPAA

BeyondA1C and MedicaMetrix may function as a Business Associate under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) when we provide services to covered healthcare entities, such as hospitals, clinics, or physician practices. In that capacity, we are required to protect any protected health information (PHI) we receive and to use it only as permitted by our business associate agreements and applicable law.

How we use and disclose PHI

As a business associate, we may use PHI only to perform our contracted services, which may include supporting device data management, research coordination, and quality reporting. We do not sell PHI. We may disclose PHI:

  • To subcontractors who assist us in providing services, under written confidentiality agreements
  • As required by law, including to public health authorities or law enforcement when legally required
  • To the covered entity we serve, at their direction
  • For our own legal, compliance, or administrative activities, to the extent permitted by HIPAA

Safeguards

We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect PHI from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure. Our security practices are reviewed regularly to ensure compliance with the HIPAA Security Rule.

Your rights

Your rights regarding your health information are primarily managed by your healthcare provider, who is the covered entity under HIPAA. If you have questions about your health records, please contact your provider directly. If you believe your privacy rights have been violated in connection with our services, you may contact us or file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services Office for Civil Rights at hhs.gov/ocr.

Contact

For questions about our HIPAA compliance or to report a concern, contact us at privacy@beyonda1c.com or at the address below.

Cookie Policy

How we use cookies

Last updated: April 2025

What are cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, understand how visitors use the site, and improve the overall experience. Cookies can be session-based (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent (stored for a set period).

What we use

  • Essential cookies: Required for the website to function. These enable basic navigation, form submission, and security features. You cannot opt out of these without significantly impairing site functionality.
  • Analytics cookies: We use tools such as Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our site, which pages are most visited, and where traffic originates. This data is aggregated and anonymized. It helps us improve the site over time.
  • Preference cookies: Used to remember settings or choices you make on the site, such as navigation state.

We do not use advertising cookies or third-party tracking for retargeting purposes.

Managing cookies

Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies through their settings. Disabling cookies may limit some website functionality. For instructions on managing cookies in common browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Do Not Track

Some browsers transmit a Do Not Track (DNT) signal. Our website does not currently alter its behavior in response to DNT signals, as there is no uniform standard for interpreting them. We will revisit this as standards evolve.

Your Data Rights

Access, correction, and deletion

Last updated: April 2025

Your general rights

Regardless of where you live, you have meaningful rights over your personal information. We honor the following requests from individuals who interact with us:

  • Access: You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correction: You may request that we correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Deletion: You may request that we delete your personal information, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Opt-out: You may opt out of receiving marketing communications at any time by emailing us or clicking unsubscribe in any email we send.

California residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

  • The right to know what personal information we collect, use, disclose, or sell
  • The right to delete personal information we have collected about you
  • The right to correct inaccurate personal information
  • The right to opt out of the sale or sharing of personal information (we do not sell personal information)
  • The right to limit use of sensitive personal information
  • The right to non-discrimination for exercising your privacy rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@beyonda1c.com. We will respond within 45 days of receiving a verifiable request.

Nevada residents

Nevada residents may opt out of the sale of personal information. We do not sell personal information, but you may submit a request at privacy@beyonda1c.com for our records.

How to submit a request

Email privacy@beyonda1c.com with the subject line "Data Rights Request" and include your name, email address, the nature of your request, and, if applicable, the state you reside in. We will verify your identity before processing your request and respond within the timeframe required by applicable law.
