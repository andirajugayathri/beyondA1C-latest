BDRI Finding 01

A1C measures average glucose. It cannot reveal what is happening to your patients' nerves, eyes, and kidneys.

BDRI's first breakthrough was documenting the specific ways A1C fails as a standalone measure of diabetic complication risk.

Limitation 01
A1C misses silent, early damage

Up to 50% of patients with diabetic neuropathy are completely asymptomatic in the early and moderate stages. Nerve damage accumulates for years with no signal in a standard blood panel. By the time symptoms appear, the window for meaningful intervention has often closed.

↗ Damage begins years before any clinical sign
Limitation 02
Good A1C does not mean no complications

A province-wide study from Alberta Health Services found that over one-third of foot ulcer and amputation patients had an A1C below 7%, clinically considered good control. Glycemic control matters, but it does not predict who develops tissue damage.

↗ Source: Alberta Health Services Diabetes Foot Care Pathway, 2016
Limitation 03
A1C does not screen for microvascular damage

A1C reflects glycated hemoglobin. It does not measure nerve conduction, retinal vascular integrity, or kidney filtration. Those are separate biological processes that need direct measurement. Relying on A1C alone leaves the most important window for early intervention completely dark.

↗ Late-detected complications cost $20,000–$80,000 per case
BDRI Finding 02 + 03 / How BeyondA1C Works

Peripheral neuropathy as a sentinel marker. Screened at the point of care.

BDRI's second key finding was that diabetic peripheral neuropathy is not just one complication among many. It is a sentinel marker: the first sign of a broader microvascular process that, left undetected, leads to retinopathy, nephropathy, and heart disease. BeyondA1C was built on that insight. Its evolving algorithm uses sural nerve function data to screen for the full range of microvascular complications in a single brief in-office test. No specialist training needed. BDRI Finding 02 / The Sentinel Marker

One marker. A window into the full spectrum of microvascular disease.

BDRI's research showed that diabetic peripheral neuropathy shares its root causes with retinopathy, nephropathy, and cardiovascular autonomic neuropathy: oxidative stress, endothelial dysfunction, and chronic inflammation. That is why nerve function data is not just a neuropathy screen. It is a window into the health of the entire microvascular system.

BDRI Finding 01: A1C Limitations, HbA1c Variability, and Glycemic Control in Neuropathy

These studies form the scientific foundation of BDRI's first discovery: that HbA1c is a necessary but insufficient measure of diabetic complication risk. Together they demonstrate that glycemic variability, oxidative stress, and other metabolic factors drive nerve damage in ways that average glucose cannot capture.

For Patients

Screening for complications before they become serious.

BeyondA1C is a quick, painless in-office test that screens for diabetic microvascular complications: nerve damage, kidney disease risk, and vision loss risk, often years before any symptoms appear. If your doctor has ordered this test, here is what you should know.

What is BeyondA1C and why would my doctor order it? +
BeyondA1C uses a small handheld device to measure how well the sural nerve in your ankle conducts electrical signals. Because peripheral neuropathy is one of the first signs of microvascular disease, this short test gives your doctor a useful window into your overall complication risk, including neuropathy, kidney disease, and eye disease.
Is the test painful or time-consuming? +
Not at all. The full test takes under one minute. You might feel a brief, mild tingling, a bit like when a foot falls asleep. Most patients are surprised by how quick it is, and there is nothing you need to do beforehand.
Can I have complications if my A1C is in a good range? +
Yes, and that is exactly why BeyondA1C was developed. Research from Alberta Health Services found that over one-third of patients who developed a foot ulcer or had an amputation had an A1C below 7%, which is considered well-controlled. Managing blood glucose matters a great deal, but A1C cannot tell your doctor whether your nerves, kidneys, or eyes are already being affected. BeyondA1C addresses that gap.
What happens if complications are found early? +
Finding complications early opens up real options: tighter glucose management, lifestyle changes, targeted medications, foot care plans, and timely referrals to the right specialists before damage becomes irreversible. The earlier things are caught, the more can be done.
Who should be screened with BeyondA1C? +
BeyondA1C is worth considering for anyone living with type 1 or type 2 diabetes, especially if you have had diabetes for five or more years, are over 40, have metabolic syndrome, high blood pressure, or high cholesterol, take metformin (which can deplete B12 and contribute to neuropathy), or have never had a neuropathy screening. Ask your primary care doctor, endocrinologist, or podiatrist whether it is right for you.

About Us

Boston Diabetes Research Institute: where BeyondA1C was born.

The Boston Diabetes Research Institute (BDRI) is a clinical research and innovation division of MedicaMetrix, focused on advancing diabetic complication detection. BDRI's research identified three interconnected findings that led to the creation of BeyondA1C.

BDRI first documented the specific limitations of A1C as a standalone measure of microvascular risk. It then established that peripheral neuropathy functions as a sentinel marker, predicting the full cascade of complications including retinopathy, nephropathy, and cardiovascular autonomic neuropathy. Building on those two findings, BDRI developed BeyondA1C: a point-of-care screening test powered by an evolving algorithm that uses sural nerve function data to generate a complication risk profile.

BeyondA1C runs on a handheld, FDA-cleared nerve conduction device. It is not a symptom checklist or a scoring model. It is a direct physiological measurement of nerve conduction, grounded in more than 35 peer-reviewed studies and validated in over 2 million patient tests worldwide.

We work with health systems, ACOs, endocrinology practices, and population health programs to bring this innovation into standard diabetes care. We also support investigator-initiated research and clinical trials. BeyondA1C, developed by the Boston Diabetes Research Institute, is focused on making early microvascular complication screening accessible and affordable in every primary care clinic in the world.
